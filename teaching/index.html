Teaching

Undergraduate Course

Field Practice in Biology

A hands-on undergraduate course introducing students to biological field methods with a focus on macrofungal diversity. Students learn specimen collection techniques, morphological identification of mushrooms and other macrofungi, and ecological surveying in Korean forest ecosystems. The course bridges classroom taxonomy with real-world biodiversity assessment.

  • Specimen collection and preservation protocols
  • Macro- and micro-morphological identification methods
  • Forest ecosystem ecology and habitat assessment
  • Field data recording and voucher documentation
  • Introduction to fungal diversity in temperate forests
Outstanding Teaching Award, 2019
Public Outreach

Field Taxonomy Training Program for Citizen Scientists

A public outreach program designed to train citizen scientists in fungal field identification. Participants develop practical skills to contribute to large-scale mycological surveys, expanding biodiversity monitoring capacity beyond institutional boundaries. The program emphasizes safe foraging practices, accurate species identification, and responsible data contribution.

  • Introduction to macrofungal diversity and ecology
  • Safe identification of edible vs. toxic species
  • Field data recording and citizen science platforms

  • Conservation ethics and ecosystem stewardship
