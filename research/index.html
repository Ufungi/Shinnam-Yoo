Functionally distinct core microbes of Tricholoma matsutake revealed by cross-study analysis

Yoo, S., Seo, C. W. & Lim, Y. W.

Through cross-study phylogenetic analysis of T. matsutake shiro microbiomes compiled from geographically diverse datasets, this study identifies core microbial phylotypes conserved across shiro environments alongside niche-specific microbes associated with distinct microhabitats (soil, colonized roots, fruitbodies). These associates perform key functions including degradation of aromatic compounds and fruitbody development, providing a foundation for improving matsutake cultivation strategies.

Exploring Fine-Scale Assembly of Ectomycorrhizal Fungal Communities Through Phylogenetic and Spatial Distribution Analyses

Yoo, S., Cho, Y., Park, K. H. & Lim, Y. W.

Compares ectomycorrhizal fungal community assembly on pine seedlings between disturbed habitats and mature Korean forests using phylogenetic and spatial analyses. In disturbed areas, phylogenetically restricted lineages dominated and similar species aggregated at the seedling scale, while root-tip-level analysis revealed spatial segregation consistent with limiting similarity as an assembly mechanism. Mature forest communities showed no distinct phylogenetic signal, indicating that assembly mechanisms vary by habitat and spatial scale.

Fourteen Unrecorded Species of Agaricales Underw. (Agaricomycetes, Basidiomycota) from the Republic of Korea

Yoo, S., Cho, Y., Kim, J. S., Kim, M. & Lim, Y. W.

Reports 14 Agaricales species newly recorded for the Republic of Korea, collected between 2018 and 2020 and spanning nine genera including Cortinarius, Inocybe, and Hygrocybe. Each species was identified by molecular phylogenetic analysis of ITS sequences combined with microscopic examination, establishing reliable reference descriptions that improve upon previous morphology-only identifications.
